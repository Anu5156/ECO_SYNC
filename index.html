Topic: Carbon Footprint

Understanding Carbon Scopes: 1, 2, and 3

Greenhouse gas emissions are categorized into three groups or 'scopes' by the Greenhouse Gas Protocol. Scope 1 represents direct emissions from sources owned or controlled (such as fuel combusted in your car). Scope 2 represents indirect emissions from purchased electricity, steam, or heating. Scope 3 represents all other indirect emissions throughout the value chain of goods, food, and flights you buy. Reducing Scope 3 emissions often has the largest impact on a personal footprint.

Topic: Scientific Benchmarks

Why is 2 Tons the Golden Target?

Under the Paris Agreement, nations have committed to limiting global warming to well below 2°C (ideally 1.5°C) to avoid severe climate disruption. To achieve this, global greenhouse gas emissions must drop significantly. By 2050, the average personal carbon footprint worldwide must fall to under 2 tons of CO₂e per year. Currently, the global average stands at 4 tons, with high-income countries averaging upwards of 12-16 tons per person.

Topic: Daily Impact

The Hidden Carbon in Food Systems

Food accounts for nearly 26% of global greenhouse gas emissions. However, not all foods are equal. Producing one kilogram of beef releases approximately 60 kilograms of greenhouse gases, while producing a kilogram of peas releases less than 1 kilogram. Shifting to a vegetarian or vegan diet, or simply incorporating meatless days, is one of the most effective and direct levers individuals have to lower their carbon footprint.
